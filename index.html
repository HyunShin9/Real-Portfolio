About Me

Recently, I had the privilege to attend and graduate from the University Of Georgia, and there, I majored in Housing and successfully earned a bachelors by May 2018. During my time within the college, I found a rising interest within the social branding that interconnects communities to their residents, and saw that the growing storm of social media had vastly changed the platform of advertising and broadened the inter-relations between the principal and the agent.
As my studies grew more austere I soon figured out how in depth the housing communities relied on websites and local data to make accurate predictions within the market, and because of this, I was motivated to delve into web design. Durning the entry time of my finals, I enlisted into a coding boot camp hosted by, none other than, Georgia Tech.

While attending college, during my second year, I took a break to earn my real-estate license, and when I resumed my education, I found it rather challenging to maintain my continuing education hours with the back and forth of the seminars I attended within the school.
However, through all of this, I found that I enjoyed learning, and realized that we, as people, go through life while in a constant state of education. Now, when I finished college I made the choice to continue by education by learning how to code because as Albert Einstein said, "Once you stop learning, you start dying".
